Overview

This article reviews the various educational intervention methods used in quasi-experimental studies to improve public awareness and knowledge about cancer.

Educational Methods

  • Lectures & Seminars: Facilitated discussions for clarifying doubts and enhancing understanding.
  • Brochures & Posters: Printed and online materials for passive learning and easy reference.
  • Mobile Apps (mHealth): Personalized education tools like ColorApp and BrAware to boost screening participation.
  • Videos: Interactive and youth-targeted videos to promote behavioral change.
  • Multimodal Approaches: Combining seminars, apps, videos, and games for more effective outreach.

Conclusion

Educational interventions play a crucial role in cancer knowledge and prevention. Well-designed content in accessible formats can lead to lasting behavior changes and better health outcomes.
